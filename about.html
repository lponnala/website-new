I am a quantitative analyst in the finance industry, based in New York since 2011. Over the years, I have worked in asset management, market making, risk, regulation, and index research.

Previously, I was a postdoctoral researcher at Cornell University, hosted at the Bioinformatics division of the Biotechnology Institute in Ithaca. I worked on computational problems in high-throughput genomics and quantitative proteomics in collaboration with life science researchers.

I completed my PhD at NCSU in Electrical Engineering (major) and Statistics (minor). I was a member of the Bioinformatics Lab where I developed a signal processing model for analyzing ribosomal frameshifts.
